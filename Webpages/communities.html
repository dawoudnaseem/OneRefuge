Communities for Refugees

Overview

Finding a sense of belonging in a new country can be challenging. Building connections within communities that share your language, culture, or religion can help ease the transition and create a support network. This page offers guidance on finding and connecting with communities that can provide comfort, understanding, and practical assistance.

Why Communities Matter

Communities can offer:

  • Emotional Support: Connecting with people who understand your experiences can provide comfort and reduce feelings of isolation.
  • Practical Assistance: Many communities offer help with navigating local services, finding jobs, or learning the language. Some even organize carpooling or childcare services to make life easier.
  • Cultural Continuity: Staying connected to your culture can provide a sense of stability and identity in a new environment. Festivals, cultural workshops, and language classes often help preserve these ties.
  • Opportunities for Collaboration: Communities are great places to find opportunities for volunteering, skills-sharing, or even starting group businesses with people who share your background.

How to Find Communities

  • Local Cultural Centers: Many cities have centers dedicated to specific cultures or nationalities that host events and offer support. Check your city’s local directory or online forums for details.
  • Online Platforms: Websites and social media groups, such as Facebook and Meetup, can help you find local communities. Platforms like Nextdoor are also useful for connecting with your immediate neighborhood.
  • Community Events: Attend local festivals, markets, or workshops to meet others and learn more about what’s available in your area. Libraries often have bulletin boards advertising free community events.
  • Religious Organizations: Places of worship often serve as gathering points for people from similar backgrounds and provide various forms of assistance, such as language classes, legal aid, and job referrals.

Communities in Canada

Canada is home to diverse communities that welcome newcomers with open arms. Here are some resources to explore:

  • Newcomer Services: Programs designed to help refugees integrate and find support networks.
  • MOSAIC BC: An organization that helps immigrants and refugees connect with resources and communities in British Columbia.
  • City of Toronto Community Programs: Information on community groups and events in the Greater Toronto Area.
  • Nova Scotia Community Connections: A directory for community organizations across Nova Scotia.

Key Contacts

  • Settlement.Org: A comprehensive resource for finding community services across Canada.
  • Immigrant Services Association of Nova Scotia (ISANS): Offering programs and services to help refugees and immigrants build connections.
  • YWCA Calgary: Provides support and opportunities for women and families in the Calgary area.
  • YMCA-YWCA National Network: Local YMCAs often offer settlement programs, activities, and community building events.
