Legal Rights and Support for Refugees

Overview

Understanding your legal rights as a refugee is crucial to ensuring your safety and stability in your new home. This guide provides information on your legal protections, the asylum process, and resources for legal support in Canada and beyond.

Legal Rights of Refugees

As a refugee, you are entitled to specific legal protections under international and national laws:

  • UN Refugee Convention: Refugees are protected under the 1951 Refugee Convention, which guarantees basic rights such as freedom from persecution and access to housing, education, and public relief.
  • Non-Refoulement Principle: This principle ensures that you cannot be returned to a country where your life or freedom would be at risk.
  • Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms: In Canada, refugees are entitled to fundamental rights, including equality and freedom from discrimination.

The Asylum Process

Seeking asylum can be complex, but understanding the process can help you navigate it more confidently:

  • Making a Claim: You can make a refugee claim at a port of entry or online through the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) portal.
  • Eligibility Review: Your claim will be reviewed to determine if you meet the criteria for refugee status.
  • Hearing with the IRB: If eligible, your case will be presented to the Immigration and Refugee Board (IRB), where a decision will be made.

Legal Support Resources

Accessing legal assistance is critical to navigating the asylum process and understanding your rights:

  • Legal Aid Ontario: Offers free or low-cost legal assistance to refugees in Ontario.
  • Canadian Council for Refugees: Provides resources and advocacy for refugee rights in Canada.
  • Amnesty International Canada: Offers guidance on refugee protection and human rights.
