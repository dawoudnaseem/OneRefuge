Housing Support for Refugees

Overview

Securing safe and affordable housing is one of the first and most important steps for refugees as they begin rebuilding their lives. This guide provides resources and tips to help you find temporary and permanent housing in Canada.

Types of Housing

Understanding the different types of housing available can help you make the best choice for your needs:

  • Temporary Housing: Shelters and hostels provide short-term accommodation for refugees who need immediate assistance.
  • Rental Housing: Apartments or shared housing are more stable options for long-term living.
  • Supportive Housing: This option combines affordable housing with support services such as counseling and job training.

Resources for Finding Housing

These organizations and programs can help you secure housing:

  • Canadian Red Cross: Offers temporary housing and support for refugees during emergencies.
  • Homeless Hub: Provides information on shelters and housing programs across Canada.
  • Settlement.Org: A resource for finding affordable housing and understanding your tenant rights.
  • YWCA Canada: Offers housing programs specifically designed for women and families.

Tenant Rights in Canada

Knowing your rights as a tenant can help you feel secure in your housing arrangement:

  • You have the right to a safe and well-maintained home.
  • Your landlord must provide you with a written lease agreement that outlines the terms of your rental.
  • Rent increases must comply with provincial guidelines and cannot be arbitrary.
  • You have the right to privacy; landlords must provide notice before entering your home.

Tips for Securing Housing

Here are some practical tips to help you find and secure housing:

  • Start with temporary housing while you explore long-term options.
  • Contact local immigrant services for referrals to housing programs.
  • Use online platforms like Kijiji and Craigslist to search for rental listings.
  • Be prepared with references and proof of income when applying for rentals.

Emergency Housing

If you are in immediate need of housing, contact these organizations:

  • The Salvation Army: Provides emergency shelter and transitional housing services.
  • Covenant House: Offers shelter and support for young refugees and families.
  • Reaching Home: A federal program addressing homelessness in Canada.
